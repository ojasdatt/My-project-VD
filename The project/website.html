Unravel
(Logical-puzzle)

A puzzle-platformer starring Yarny, a small creature made of red yarn traversing environments inspired by Scandinavian landscapes to reconnect lost memories. You have to navigate physics-based challenges, using Yarny’s own thread to swing, pull, and bridge gaps, while managing how much yarn is left to avoid becoming too frail to progress!

Puzzle 1: Chapter 3: Berry Mire: Considered by many to be one of the early difficult spots. A specific, tricky puzzle involves a heavy log that requires careful positioning and untying of yarn to move, leading to potential frustration.

Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐ |
Time - 6 minutes #for casual player|
Type: positioning, water style|

Risks: Drowning, yarn tanglement.

Just playing, or solving this game will enhance your logical skills, and allow you to solve almost any in-game challenge like this.

What to do?

1. Turn the branch into a raft and prep the water
Break the hanging branch so it falls into the water, creating the log raft.
Tie Yarny to the nearby lever/peg and raise the water level so the log can float freely instead of snagging on the edge.

2. Anchor first, then release
Before riding the log, tie it briefly to a ground hook so it doesn’t drift away.
Once the water is high enough, untie the log and immediately jump on—this timing prevents the log from getting stuck or moving too fast without you.

3. Retrieve and reuse the log
After crossing, lasso or drag the log out of the water and reposition it.
You’ll need the same log again to climb over the rock and continue through the swamp.

Dont: One common mistake to avoid 🚫; Releasing the log before the water is fully raised.

Test your puzzle-solving skills! (hint: The heaviest one is not the lightest color!)

The 3x3 Grid

The crafting table allows you to use all the crafting recipes in Minecraft.
There are a lot of basic items that you can craft without a crafting table, like blocks for building, tools for mining, and armor for safety (and style!).
But whether you’re crafting from your inventory or at a crafting table, you will always need two things: a crafting recipe, and supplies!

How to do it!

Minecraft’s world is full of materials you can combine to craft new items.
You can follow the recipe book or experiment on your own.
To make a crafting table, you need four wooden planks—so your first task is crafting planks themselves.
Planks come from wood, which you get by punching trees until wood blocks drop.
Walking near the dropped blocks automatically collects them into your inventory.
While punching works for wood, most other resources require proper tools, like pickaxes for ore or buckets for water.
Once you have wood, open your inventory to access the crafting grid.
Place a wood block into the 2×2 grid to craft four planks.
With those planks, you now have everything needed to craft a crafting table.

Essential Tools & Their Uses

Tools help you gather resources faster and unlock new materials. Using the right tool saves time and prevents items from breaking or dropping nothing.

What each tool does

• Pickaxe – Mines stone, ores, and important blocks like diamonds.
• Axe – Cuts wood faster and deals strong melee damage.
• Shovel – Speeds up digging dirt, sand, gravel, and snow.
• Hoe – Prepares farmland for crops.
• Sword – Best for fighting mobs and breaking certain blocks like bamboo.

How to craft tools

All tools follow the same recipe pattern: 2 sticks for the handle + the material on top (wood, stone, iron, gold, or diamond).

For example, a wooden pickaxe uses:
• 3 wooden planks across the top row
• 2 sticks in the center column

Upgrading to stone, iron, or diamond makes tools last longer and mine faster.

Pro Tip

Never mine diamonds with a wooden or stone pickaxe — they will break and drop nothing. Always use at least an iron pickaxe.

Landing & Early Game

Your first decisions in Fortnite decide the pace of the entire match.
Landing near loot-rich areas gives you a strong start, but landing slightly outside hotspots lets you gear up safely.
Always prioritize shields—surviving the first fight is more important than chasing eliminations.

How to do it!

Pick a landing spot with at least 3–4 chests nearby. Glide lower than other players to reach the ground first.
Once you land, grab any weapon immediately—even a pistol is better than nothing.
After looting, pop shield potions right away. A player with shields almost always wins early trades.

Building Basics

Building is Fortnite’s signature mechanic. Even simple builds can save your life.
A single wall can block a sniper shot, and a quick ramp gives you height advantage in close fights.

How to do it!

Start with the essentials:
• Place a wall when you're shot at.
• Build a ramp to gain height.
• Use a 1×1 tower (wall–ramp–floor) to reset fights or heal safely.
As you improve, practice combining edits and builds to outmaneuver opponents.

Early Progression

Clash of Clans rewards smart upgrades, not rushing. Upgrading your army camps, spell factory, and barracks early gives you stronger attacks and faster training times.
A strong offense makes farming resources much easier.

How to do it!

Focus on these upgrades first:
• Army Camps (more troops = stronger raids)
• Barracks & Spell Factory (unlock better strategies)
• Clan Castle (join a clan for donated troops)
Avoid upgrading your Town Hall too early—stronger enemies will crush you.

Basic Attack Strategy

Every good attack starts with funneling. This means clearing buildings on the sides so your main troops go straight into the base instead of walking around it.

How to do it!

1. Use a few troops to clear outside buildings on the left and right.
2. Deploy your main army in the center so they move into the core.
3. Save your heroes and spells for the toughest defenses.
4. Drop cleanup troops last to secure extra stars.

As you level up, experiment with strategies like Giants + Wizards, Dragons, or Hog Riders depending on your Town Hall level.
